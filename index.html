Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India"

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the "Missile Man of India," left an indelible mark on India's scientific, technological, and educational landscape. He is very simple person who lived an unpretentious lifestyle. He had a keen interest in literature and wrote poems. He never married. He always faced media himself for his failures while let others address for the success. He remained active till the very end. He died while delivering a lecture at the IIM.

The Timeline

  • 1931: Born on 15 October, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from University of Madras.
  • 1960: Graduated in Aerospace Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969: Transferred to ISRO from the DRDO.
  • 1980: Was involved in the development of several Indegenous Missiles for India like Agni, Prithvi.
  • 1981: Awarded Padma Bhushan.
  • 1990: Awarded Padma Vibhushan.
  • 1992: Chief Advisor for the India's Nuclear Program.
  • 1997: Awarded Bharat Ratna.
  • 2002: Became 11th President of India.
  • 2015:He passed away on July 27, 2015 after collapsing during a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management in Shillong.
